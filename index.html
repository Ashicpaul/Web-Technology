Zoo Animal info

Animal Type

Mammals:This group includes familiar zoo favorites.
Examples: Lions, tigers, elephants, giraffes, zebras, rhinoceroses, bears, monkeys, pandas, leopards, dolphins, kangaroos, koalas, etc.
Birds: Zoos often feature a vibrant array of birds.
Examples: Eagles, parrots, peacocks, toucans, ostriches, penguins, owls, pelicans, macaws, hornbills, kingfishers, hummingbirds, etc.
Reptiles: You'll encounter a variety of reptiles in dedicated reptile houses or enclosures.
Examples: Snakes (like boas and pythons), lizards (chameleons, geckos, iguanas), crocodiles, alligators, turtles, tortoises, Komodo dragons, etc.
Amphibians: These creatures often reside in dedicated exhibits or amphibian centers.
Examples: Frogs, salamanders.
Fish: Aquariums, often part of zoos, display diverse aquatic life.
Examples: Sharks, eels, stingrays, angelfish, seahorses, piranhas, etc.

Habitats

Terrestrial habitats (land-based)

  1. Forests:

    2. Tropical Rainforests: Characterized by high temperatures and humidity, abundant rainfall, and a huge diversity of plant and animal life. They are found near the equator in areas like South America, Africa, Asia, and Australia. Examples: Tigers, orangutans, monkeys, jaguars, gorillas. Temperate Forests: Exhibit four distinct seasons and are home to both evergreen and deciduous trees. Examples: Deer, bears, wolves, squirrels, woodpeckers. Boreal Forests (Taiga): Cold and characterized by long, harsh winters. Examples: Elk, lemmings, grizzly bears, wolves.

  2. Grasslands:

    3. Description: Wide-open spaces with grasses and few trees. Climate: Moderate rainfall; dry seasons. Animals Found: Elephants, Bison, Antelope.

  3. Ocean

    4. Description: Saltwater environments including seas and oceans. Climate: Varies by region (tropical to polar). Animals Found: Dolphins, Sharks, Whales.

Animal Diets

  1. Mammals

    2. Herbivores: Elephants, Deer (eat grass, leaves, fruits) Carnivores: Lions, Tigers (eat other animals) Omnivores: Bears, Monkeys (eat fruits, insects, small animals, fish)

  2. Birds

    3. Herbivores: Parrots (eat seeds, fruits, nuts) Carnivores: Eagles, Owls (eat small mammals, fish) Omnivores: Crows, Ducks (eat plants, insects, small fish)

  3. Reptiles

    4. Carnivores: Snakes, Crocodiles, Lizards (eat rodents, birds, insects, fish) Some eat eggs, small animals, or even other reptiles.

  4. Fish

    5. Carnivores: Sharks, Piranhas (eat other fish, crustaceans) Herbivores: Parrotfish, Surgeonfish (eat algae and aquatic plants) Omnivores: Goldfish, Guppies (eat plants, insects, small crustaceans)

  5. Amphibians

    6. Mostly Carnivores or Insectivores: Frogs, Salamanders, Newts (eat insects, worms, small fish) Tadpoles (young frogs) are sometimes herbivores (eat algae)
